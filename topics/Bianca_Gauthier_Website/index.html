Information About Disabilities

Introduction

Welcome to the information section of this website. Here, you can explore more about the disabilities that influence my work, studies, and creative processes. The aim is to create awareness and share my personal journey in navigating these challenges.

As I navigate a landscape filled with ongoing tests and the constant adjustment of medications, I invite you to join me in this exploration. My journey is marked by resilience, creativity, and the pursuit of understanding—an endeavor that is both deeply personal and universally relevant. Here, you'll find insights into how these disabilities interconnect and impact my life, along with links to additional resources for each condition, so you can further your understanding of these experiences.

Living with Disabilities - An Interactive Experience

Welcome to my interactive experimental website, a space dedicated to exploring the complexities of living with various disabilities, including chronic migraines, postherpetic neuralgia, ADHD, dyslexia, and high anxiety. Each of these conditions has shaped my personal and creative journey in profound ways, influencing my daily life, education, and artistic expression. Through candid storytelling and informative resources, I aim to shed light on the unique challenges and experiences that come with each disability.

This website is an interactive experience demonstrating the daily effects of certain disabilities I am diagnosed with. The experience is designed to be immersive and may affect users differently. While not meant to elicit pity or be completely comprehensive, it offers a glimpse into my personal experience with these conditions.

The purpose of this website is for it to be an experience that users will not be able to stop completely. The experience is not planned to be good or bad, as it can affect people differently. The purpose is not to govern pity or to be fully informative. This website is trying to demonstrate some of the effects that I have from my disabilities, however, it isn't completely informative as I acknowledge that not everyone with any of these disabilities has the same experience. I will put links for each of these disabilities to websites that are informative if you would like to learn more in detail about any of these disabilities.

Warnings & Navigation

Please note that this website is not intended to be a diagnostic tool or a source of professional advice. Each person’s experience with disabilities is unique, and the simulations and content here are based solely on my personal perspective. Use the navigation menu or the buttons on the home page to explore more.

ADHD

ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder) affects focus, organization, and energy regulation. It significantly impacts my work by making it challenging to stay on track, especially with detailed projects.

My personal experience with ADHD includes hyper-focus on creative projects and difficulty managing mundane tasks. This has shaped how I approach art and coding, blending spontaneity with structure.

Living with ADHD presents a constant balancing act, where focus and organization can often feel elusive. The challenge intensifies when medication no longer works as expected, requiring ongoing experimentation to find the right balance. Dyslexia

Dyslexia primarily affects reading, writing, and sometimes coding. I often encounter errors in syntax and spelling, which require extra effort to identify and correct.

My unique journey with dyslexia has led me to develop visual techniques and methods to minimize its impact on my creative and academic work.

Dyslexia shapes my approach to both traditional art and new ventures like coding. While it can enhance creative thinking, the intricacies of language processing make navigating technical subjects particularly challenging.

Chronic Migraines

Migraines cause severe pain, sensitivity to light, and interruptions in productivity. Frequent migraines force me to adapt my workflow and manage my time around unpredictable episodes.

Art becomes both a refuge and a challenge during migraine episodes. While it helps process emotions, the physical strain can be overwhelming.

Chronic migraines are more than just headaches; they are a debilitating condition that can disrupt everyday life, especially in educational and artistic pursuits.
