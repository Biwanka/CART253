My Disability-Inspired Artwork

What is That?

This painting subject is of the memory, from when I was a child, of discovering that I had learning disabilities. Portraying this out-of-body experience of fear until a minute later, I realized I didn't know what they meant. Bringing forth this absurd alienation that is put towards the label of 'different' when it's not a bad thing.

The Unknown

Digital drawing educates people on ADHD. Many people do not know about learning disability therefore they act harshly. I have ADHD and have been labelled and shunned for it. ADHD does not make me different and I want to make people aware of this with my drawing.

Invisible Purple Acceptance

This painting is a self-portrait. This painting depicts some of my non-visual disabilities. I have chronic Migraines and Nerve Damage from having shingles at the age of 19. Specifically, these two disabilities affect my day-to-day life, as they affect my productivity and more. Therefore, with art, I have to adapt. With art and my family, I have been able to adapt to these new changes, however, these are still daily pains that I live with. I have come to an acceptance of these new changes and this painting doesn't show how painful it is. This demonstrates my tiredness and acceptance. The purple me helps depict the look of my migraines and nerves, I also wanted to depict the specific places affected by these small nerve-like lines.(Bianca Gauthier, September 2023, 30x40 inch, Acrylic Painting)

The Unseen Mind

Description: This artwork captures the fragmented thought patterns caused by ADHD, with scattered, vibrant shapes symbolizing the energy and disarray of the mind.

Title: Deepest Fear

This represents my fear of failure. I have been to a low point when I was younger because of my learning disabilities. I have worked hard to the top, but when I went into art with no experience, I was scared to return to the bottom.This work utilizes graphite to create contrast between a range of dark tones
