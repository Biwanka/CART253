Living with Disabilities - An Interactive Experience

This website is an interactive experience demonstrating the daily effects of certain disabilities I am diagnosed with. The experience is designed to be immersive and may affect users differently. While not meant to elicit pity or be completely comprehensive, it offers a glimpse into my personal experience with these conditions.

The purpose of this website is for it to be an experience that users will not be able to stop completely. The experience is not planned to be good or bad, as it can affect people differently. The purpose is not to govern pity or to be fully informative. This website is trying to demonstrate some of the effects that I have from my disabilities, however, it isn't completely informative as I acknowledge that not everyone with any of these disabilities has the same experience. I will put links for each of these disabilities to websites that are informative if you would like to learn more in detail about any of these disabilities.

Welcome to my interactive experimental website, a space dedicated to exploring the complexities of living with various disabilities, including chronic migraines, postherpetic neuralgia, ADHD, dyslexia, and high anxiety. Each of these conditions has shaped my personal and creative journey in profound ways, influencing my daily life, education, and artistic expression. Through candid storytelling and informative resources, I aim to shed light on the unique challenges and experiences that come with each disability.

As I navigate a landscape filled with ongoing tests and the constant adjustment of medications, I invite you to join me in this exploration. My journey is marked by resilience, creativity, and the pursuit of understanding—an endeavor that is both deeply personal and universally relevant. Here, you'll find insights into how these disabilities interconnect and impact my life, along with links to additional resources for each condition, so you can further your understanding of these experiences.

ADHD

Living with ADHD presents a constant balancing act, where focus and organization can often feel elusive. The challenge intensifies when medication no longer works as expected, requiring ongoing experimentation to find the right balance. Chronic Migrains

Chronic migraines are more than just headaches; they are a debilitating condition that can disrupt everyday life, especially in educational and artistic pursuits. The unpredictable nature of migraines often leads to missed opportunities and feelings of isolation. Here are three informative links for chronic migraines, including those with aura and vestibular migraines: American Migraine Foundation - Provides comprehensive information about different types of migraines, including chronic migraines and those with aura.
